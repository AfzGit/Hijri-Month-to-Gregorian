Hijri Information

Gregorian days start when the clock hits 12 AM, but Hijri uses phases of moon to determine it's calendar.
  • Hijri days start/end at sunset (called as Magribh)
    • Example: Around 18:30 in Asia (Times vary)
  • There are 29-30 days in a hijri month.
    • 29 if the new moon is sighted
    • 30 if the new moon was not sighted

Months in Hijri Calendar:

  1. Muharram*
  2. Safar
  3. Rabi' Al-Awwal
  4. Rabi' Al-Thani
  5. Jumada Al-Awwal
  6. Jumada Al-Thani
  7. Rajab*
  8. Sha'ban
  9. Ramadan
  10. Shawwal
  11. Dhu al-Qi'dah*
  12. Dhu al-Hijjah*

---

* Holy Islamic months
